Unpacking Parental Involvement Challenging Achievement Situations

The UPICAS project is funded by the Luxembourg National Research Fund (FNR) under the CORE funding scheme, with a total budget of €697,000 (Project Reference: C23/SC/18091359). The project is hosted at the University of Luxembourg, in collaboration with Theresa Dicke from the Australian Catholic University and Guy Roth from Ben-Gurion University. UPICAS has received ethical approval from the Ethics Committee of the University of Luxembourg (Protocol Number: ERP ID 24-011 UPICAS) and from Luxembourg's Ministère de l'Education nationale, de l'Enfance et de la Jeunesse. The project collaborates closely with Elerne Forum, SCRIPT, and the Luxembourg Centre for Educational Testing (LUCET). The project comprises three studies: (a) a parent-adolescent panel study, (b) a parenting coaching study, and (c) a meta-analysis.

Panel Study

The panel study investigates the parental role in children's school and emotional development. Children's challenging achievement situations, such as failing an exam, can be stressful for both the children and their parents. The study aims to identify factors that support parents in maintaining constructive parenting practices in these demanding situations.

A total of 41 primary and secondary schools in Luxembourg participated in the three-wave parent-adolescent panel study. Approximately 1,200 students (Learning Cycles 4.1 and 6e classes in Wave 1) and their parents took part. The three waves were conducted in January/February, May/June, and October/November 2025, respectively.
